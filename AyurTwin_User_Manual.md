## 1. INTRODUCTION

### What is AyurTwin?

AyurTwin is an intelligent Ayurvedic health management platform that combines traditional Ayurvedic wisdom with modern IoT technology and artificial intelligence. The system provides personalized health recommendations based on your individual constitution (prakriti) and current health status.

### Core Features

- Real-time health monitoring through connected smartwatch devices
- Personalized Ayurvedic lifestyle recommendations
- AI-powered disease risk prediction
- Customized food and ritual recommendations based on season
- Yoga and meditation guidance
- Family health monitoring capabilities
- Community engagement and health tracking
- Comprehensive health reports and analytics

### System Requirements

- Smartphone (iOS or Android)
- Internet connection (WiFi or mobile data)
- Optional: AyurTwin Band Pro smartwatch for continuous monitoring
- Minimum 2GB RAM for smooth app performance

## 7. LIFESTYLE RECOMMENDATIONS

### 7.1 Daily Routines (Dinacharya)

Dinacharya refers to the Ayurvedic daily routine customized to your constitution.

#### Morning Routine (5:00 AM - 8:00 AM)

**Wake-up Time**
- Vata types: 6:00-7:00 AM (grounding routine)
- Pitta types: 5:30-6:30 AM (cooling routine)
- Kapha types: 5:00-6:00 AM (stimulating routine)

**Tongue Scraping**
- Upon waking, before eating or drinking
- Removes toxins accumulated overnight
- Use metal or plastic scraper, gentle strokes from back to front

**Oil Massage (Abhyanga)**
- Vata: Warm sesame oil, 10-15 minutes
- Pitta: Coconut oil, 5-10 minutes
- Kapha: Mustard or ginger oil, 5-10 minutes
- Promotes circulation, calms the nervous system

**Bathing**
- Warm water for all types
- Soap for cleansing, oil not needed if massage was done
- Includes hair and teeth brushing

**Exercise/Yoga**
- Duration: 20-30 minutes
- Type: Personalized based on your dosha
- After routine, before heavy meals

**Breakfast**
- Timing: 7:30-9:00 AM, when digestive fire is building
- Type: Warm, easily digestible foods
- Avoid: Heavy, cold, caffeinated drinks on empty stomach

#### Midday Routine (12:00 PM - 1:00 PM)

**Lunch Time**
- Main meal of the day (biggest meal)
- Best between 11 AM - 1 PM when digestive fire is strongest
- Should be warm, freshly cooked, balanced with all six tastes
- Eat in peaceful environment

**Post-Lunch Activity**
- Light walk for 10-15 minutes aids digestion
- Not suitable for strenuous exercise
- Allows food to move through digestive tract naturally

#### Evening Routine (5:00 PM - 10:00 PM)

**Dinner Time**
- 2-3 hours before bedtime
- Lighter than lunch, easier to digest
- Earlier eating (6:00-7:00 PM) is better for sleep

**Evening Relaxation**
- Light activities recommended
- Avoid: Intense work, arguments, stimulating entertainment
- Suitable: Reading, gentle music, family time

**Preparation for Sleep**
- Dim lights 1 hour before bed
- Warm milk with ashwagandha or brahmi
- Light oil massage on feet and head
- Create cool, dark sleep environment

**Bedtime**
- Ideal: 10:00 PM
- Aim for 7-9 hours sleep
- Consistent bedtime helps regulate body clock

### 7.2 Seasonal Recommendations (Ritucharya)

The app adjusts recommendations based on the current season and your location.

#### Spring (March - May)
**Season Characteristics**: Cool, wet, heavy
**Dosha Imbalance**: Kapha tends to increase
**General Recommendations**:
- Increase physical activity
- Dry body massage with warm oils
- Eat light, warm, spicy foods
- Reduce dairy and heavy foods
- Herbal teas: Ginger, turmeric

#### Summer (June - August)
**Season Characteristics**: Hot, dry
**Dosha Imbalance**: Pitta tends to increase
**General Recommendations**:
- Avoid excessive sun exposure
- Cooling exercises (swimming preferred)
- Coconut oil massage
- Sweet, bitter, astringent tastes preferred
- Avoid alcohol and heating spices
- Herbal teas: Cooling herbs like mint, fennel

#### Autumn (September - November)
**Season Characteristics**: Dry, windy, light
**Dosha Imbalance**: Vata tends to increase
**General Recommendations**:
- Warm oil massage essential
- Grounding, warming exercises
- Warm, nourishing foods
- Regular meal times (crucial)
- Herbal teas: Warming spices like cinnamon

#### Winter (December - February)
**Season Characteristics**: Cold, heavy
**Dosha Imbalance**: Kapha increases, Vata variable
**General Recommendations**:
- Warming exercises essential
- Warm oil massage with stimulating oils
- Heavier foods acceptable if digestion is strong
- More sleep acceptable
- Herbal teas: Warming spices, ginger

### 7.3 Personalized Yoga Recommendations

The Lifestyle tab provides yoga and meditation practices tailored to your constitution.

#### Three Categories of Practices

**Morning Yoga**
- Best practiced 5:30-7:00 AM
- Duration: 20-30 minutes
- Energizing for the day ahead
- For Vata: Grounding poses, slower pace
- For Pitta: Cooling poses, slower pace
- For Kapha: Stimulating poses, faster pace

**Evening Yoga**
- Best practiced 6:00-7:00 PM
- Duration: 20-30 minutes
- Calming for better sleep
- For all types: Gentle, grounding practices

**Therapeutic Yoga**
- For specific health conditions
- Varying durations
- Modified poses for safety
- Examples: Yoga for stress, for back pain, for sleep

#### Understanding Yoga Recommendations

Each recommended practice displays:

**Practice Name**: Traditional name of the asana or practice

**Type**: Asana (pose), Pranayama (breathing), Meditation, etc.

**Duration**: Time needed, usually 5-15 minutes per practice

**Intensity**: 
- Low: Beginner-friendly, gentle
- Moderate: Intermediate, some strength needed
- High: Advanced, significant strength or flexibility required

**Benefits**: Key benefits listed (e.g., "calms nervous system", "increases energy")

**Match Score**: Percentage showing how well this practice suits your constitution

## 8. DISEASE RISK MANAGEMENT

### 8.1 Understanding Risk Scores

The app analyzes 10 major health conditions and provides risk percentages.

#### Risk Interpretation

**Green (0-30%)**
- Low risk for this condition
- Maintain current healthy habits
- Regular monitoring recommended

**Yellow (30-70%)**
- Moderate risk
- Preventive actions recommended
- Increase monitoring frequency

**Red (70%+)**
- High risk
- Immediate action needed
- Consult healthcare provider
- Follow prevention protocol strictly

### 8.2 Major Conditions Tracked

**1. Diabetes**
- Risk factors: Obesity, sedentary lifestyle, high sugar consumption
- Prevention: Regular exercise, balanced diet, stress management
- Monitoring: Check fasting blood sugar monthly
- Foods to avoid: Refined carbs, sugary drinks, processed foods

**2. Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)**
- Risk factors: Excess salt, stress, obesity, sedentary lifestyle
- Prevention: Exercise, salt reduction, stress management
- Monitoring: Check BP weekly
- Foods to avoid: Salty foods, processed meats, alcoholic beverages

**3. Heart Disease**
- Risk factors: Hypertension, high cholesterol, smoking, stress
- Prevention: Regular cardio, heart-healthy diet, stress reduction
- Monitoring: Annual heart checkup recommended
- Foods to emphasize: Omega-3 rich foods, whole grains

**4. Stress & Anxiety**
- Risk factors: Work pressure, poor sleep, lack of relaxation
- Prevention: Meditation, yoga, adequate sleep, social connections
- Monitoring: Self-assess stress levels, track sleep patterns
- Activities: Regular yoga, breathing exercises, outdoors time

**5. Sleep Disorders**
- Risk factors: Irregular sleep schedule, stress, caffeine, blue light
- Prevention: Consistent bedtime, dark room, relaxation practices
- Monitoring: Track sleep duration and quality
- Habits: No screens 1 hour before bed, warm milk before sleep

**6. Respiratory Issues (Asthma)**
- Risk factors: Poor air quality, allergies, stress, dampness
- Prevention: Clean environment, allergen avoidance, respiratory yoga
- Monitoring: Track breathing and wheezing
- Practices: Pranayama exercises, steam inhalation

**7. Joint Pain & Arthritis**
- Risk factors: Aging, sedentary lifestyle, cold/damp climate
- Prevention: Gentle exercise, warm oil massage, anti-inflammatory diet
- Monitoring: Joint mobility and pain assessment
- Treatments: Regular massage, warm compresses, therapeutic yoga

**8. Obesity**
- Risk factors: High calorie intake, sedentary lifestyle, poor digestion
- Prevention: Regular exercise, balanced diet, digestive support
- Monitoring: BMI, weight tracking, body composition
- Practices: Daily movement, mindful eating, digestive spices

**9. Digestive Disorders**
- Risk factors: Irregular eating, food incompatibilities, stress
- Prevention: Regular meal times, proper food combinations, chewing thoroughly
- Monitoring: Digestion quality, energy levels after meals
- Practices: Digestive massages, herbal remedies, mindful eating

**10. Recurrent Fever/Infections**
- Risk factors: Low immunity, stress, poor sleep, poor hygiene
- Prevention: Immune-building foods, adequate sleep, stress management
- Monitoring: Temperature monitoring, infection frequency
- Practices: Immunity-building herbs, proper hygiene, adequate sleep

### 8.3 Prevention Protocols

For each high-risk condition, follow these steps:

**Step 1: Assessment**
- Understand your specific risk factors
- Review relevant health metrics
- Note any symptoms or warning signs

**Step 2: Lifestyle Modifications**
- Follow personalized recommendations
- Implement dietary changes
- Add suitable yoga and exercise
- Improve sleep and stress management

**Step 3: Monitoring**
- Check relevant health metrics regularly
- Keep health journal
- Track symptom changes
- Monitor effectiveness of changes

**Step 4: Medical Consultation**
- For high-risk conditions (70%+), consult a healthcare provider
- Get professional diagnosis before assuming condition exists
- Combine app recommendations with medical advice
- Update app with any medical diagnoses

## 10. DEVICE & SENSOR SETUP

### 10.1 AyurTwin Band Pro

#### Features of the Device
- Heart rate monitoring (24/7)
- Blood oxygen saturation (SpO2)
- Skin temperature monitoring
- 3-axis accelerometer (for activity tracking)
- 7-day battery life
- Water-resistant (not waterproof)
- Bluetooth 5.0 connectivity

#### Initial Setup

1. **Charge the Device**
   - Use provided USB cable
   - Charge until LED indicator turns green (2-3 hours)
   - Full charge indicator: All LEDs light up

2. **Pair with Smartphone**
   - Open AyurTwin app
   - Go to Settings > Devices
   - Select "Add New Device"
   - Enable Bluetooth on your phone
   - Select device from scanning list
   - Confirm pairing

3. **Proper Wearing**
   - Wear on non-dominant wrist
   - Snug but not tight (should fit one finger underneath)
   - Keep clean and dry
   - Skin contact essential for accurate readings

#### Daily Use

**Wearing Recommendations**:
- Wear during day and sleep for best tracking
- Remove during charging or water exposure
- Clean once weekly with damp cloth
- Check fit - should not be loose

**Reading Accuracy**:
- Heart rate: Most accurate during rest
- SpO2: May vary by 2-3% depending on pigmentation
- Temperature: Requires 15+ minutes of wear to stabilize
- Movement: Tracks automatically

#### Data Synchronization

**Automatic Sync**:
- Occurs every 5 seconds when worn and connected
- No manual action needed
- Requires active Bluetooth connection
- Range: Up to 30 feet from smartphone

**Manual Sync**:
- Go to Device page
- Select "Sync Now" button
- Wait for syncing to complete
- Check timestamp shows recent update

#### Battery Management

**Battery Status**:
- Check percentage on Device page
- Icon color: Green (80%+), Yellow (30-80%), Red (below 30%)

**Charging Tips**:
- Charge when below 20%
- Daily charging recommended for continuous monitoring
- Use only provided charger
- Allow 2-3 hours for full charge

**Battery Life**:
- Full charge lasts 7 days with normal use
- Shorter if continuous heart rate monitoring enabled
- Completely off after 30 days without charging

#### Troubleshooting Device

**Device Not Found During Pairing**:
- Ensure device is charged
- Restart smartphone Bluetooth
- Force close AyurTwin app and reopen
- Remove and re-add device

**Data Not Syncing**:
- Check Bluetooth is enabled
- Bring device within range
- Check for app updates
- Restart device (remove and reattach)

**Inaccurate Readings**:
- Ensure snug fit on wrist
- Check for dirt on sensor surface
- Allow 5+ minutes for stabilization
- Ensure skin is clean and dry

**Connection Drops Frequently**:
- Move away from 2.4GHz WiFi interference
- Reduce distance from phone
- Update device firmware
- Restart both device and phone

### 10.2 Manual Data Entry

If not using a device, you can manually enter health data:

**Manual Entry Process**:
1. Go to Dashboard
2. Select "Add Manual Reading"
3. Choose data type (Heart Rate, Temperature, SpO2, Weight)
4. Enter value and timestamp
5. Save

**When to Use Manual Entry**:
- During device charging

## 12. TROUBLESHOOTING & SUPPORT

### 12.1 Account Issues

**Cannot Login**
- Verify email/username spelling
- Check Caps Lock is off
- Try "Forgot Password" if password unknown
- Clear app cache and retry
- Uninstall and reinstall app if persistent

**"Account Suspended" Message**
- May indicate unusual activity detected
- Contact support team for verification
- Provide proof of identity
- Account typically restored within 24 hours

**Unable to Reset Password**
- Check spam/junk folder for reset email
- Request new link if expired (links valid 24 hours)
- Provide alternate email if primary not accessible
- Use "Contact Support" for assistance

**Cannot Create Account**
- Email may already be registered
- Username may already be taken
- Check password meets requirements (min 8 chars, 1 number, 1 special)
- Try different email address

### 12.2 Data & Syncing Issues

**Data Not Syncing**
- Check internet connection (WiFi or mobile data)
- Verify app has internet permission in settings
- Try "Manual Sync" in Settings
- Restart the app
- Restart your device

**Old Data Shown**
- Force app refresh by closing and reopening
- Check internet connection active
- Verify Bluetooth connected if using device
- Clear app cache: Settings > Apps > AyurTwin > Clear Cache

**Recommendations Keep Changing**
- Recommendations update when new health data is received
- Multiple changes indicate active monitoring
- This is normal and means system is working

**Missing Days of Data**
- Check device was worn and charged those days
- Verify Bluetooth was connected during syncing
- Manual sync may help recover missed data
- Contact support if data is critical

### 12.3 Device & Sensor Issues

**Heart Rate Reading 0 or "- -"**
- Device not making proper skin contact
- Dead sensor - try restarting device
- Ensure wrist position correct (below wrist bone)
- Clean sensor surface

**SpO2 Reading Unavailable**
- Typically indicates poor signal
- Make sure device is snug on wrist
- Keep arm still during reading
- Wait 30 seconds for stabilization
- May vary with skin tone

**Device Battery Drains Quickly**
- Disable heart rate monitoring if not needed
- Reduce screen brightness
- Close unused apps on phone
- Update device firmware
- Replace device if battery is damaged

**Cannot Pair Device**
- Fully charge device first
- Disable other Bluetooth devices temporarily
- Forget device in phone Bluetooth settings
- Re-open app and attempt pairing again
- Restart both device and smartphone

**Device Feels Hot**
- Brief warmth during charging is normal
- If hot during use, remove immediately
- Allow to cool for 15+ minutes
- Do not use until cool
- Contact support if heat persists

### 12.4 App Performance Issues

- Force close app (Settings > Apps > AyurTwin > Force Stop)
- Clear cache (Settings > Apps > AyurTwin > Clear Cache)
- Restart device
- Update app from app store
- Reinstall app if persistent

**Slow Performance**
- Check available storage (need min 200MB free)
- Close other running apps
- Clear app cache
- Update to latest app version
- Restart device

**Notifications Not Working**
- Check notifications enabled for app in Settings
- Go to Settings > Notifications > AyurTwin > ON
- Check notification sound and vibration enabled
- Restart app after enabling

**Button Taps Not Responding**
- App may be loading - wait a few seconds
- Try tapping slightly differently
- Clear app cache and reopen
- Restart phone if persistent
